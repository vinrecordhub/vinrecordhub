Car Title Check: Don't Buy a Branded Title by Mistake

A branded title can cut a car's value in half and make it hard to insure or resell. A title check by VIN flags these problems before your money is gone.

What a title brand is

A title brand is an official designation that something serious happened to a vehicle — enough that a state or insurer flagged it permanently on the title.

Common title brands

  • Salvage — declared a total loss
  • Rebuilt — salvage that was repaired and re-inspected
  • Flood — water damage
  • Lemon — repeated unfixable defects
  • Junk — not safe to return to the road

How to check by VIN

Enter the VIN at checkout; your report includes title status and any recorded brands, in about 60 seconds.

What to do if you find a brand

A brand isn't always a dealbreaker (a well-done rebuilt car can be a value), but it should mean a lower price, a thorough inspection, and a check that you can insure it.

Frequently asked questions

Is a branded title bad?

It signals significant past damage and lowers value. Some rebuilt cars are fine, but only buy one at the right (lower) price after an inspection.

Can a title brand be hidden?

"Title washing" across states happens. A VIN history check helps surface brands a seller may not mention.
